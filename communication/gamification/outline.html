1. There is something about gameplay that is hardwired into the human brain.
  2. We love progressing, getting awards, ranking on leaderboards, badges, finishing tasks, leveling up, and having fun while doing it.
  3. People only consume what entertains them.
    1. An example is the “Michelin Guide”.
      1. They produced the “ultimate guide for new motorists” in 1900 to help promote their tire business, which wasn’t selling a lot.
      2. They constantly improved it and by 1930, it was a regular on the France bestseller list and the brand “Michelin” became a prominent brand.
      3. Now, the brothers never needed to worry about selling their tires ever again.
  4. Include a bar that shows people that they have completed 20%, 50%, etc. E.g. of the onboarding process
    1. This prompts us to get to 100% because we hate not finishing something.
  5. Awards.
    1. E.g., for participating in 1, 3, 5, 10 events, etc.
    2. Completing 1 task, 3, 5, 10, etc.
    3. Submitting 1, 3, 5, 10, etc. usable ideas, etc.
    4. Onboarding 1 contributor, 2 contributors, 3 contributors, 4 contributors, 5 contributors, etc.
  6. Leaderboards.
  7. Combining the awards with leaderboards.
  8. If we let someone know that they are in 3rd place for most tasks in a working group, there is a good chance they’ll take action to get in 2nd place.
  9. The same could go for the rest of the awards.
